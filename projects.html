Style Transfer

I implemeneted the style transfer network described in the paper A Neural Algorithm of Artistic Style by Gatys, Ecker and Bethge.

The basic premise is to take two existing images - typically one image is thought of as the "content" image while the other is thought of as the "style" image - along with a third image of random noise and incrementally change the pixel values of the noisey image.

The goal of continually tweaking the pixel values of the noisey image is to have a resultant new image which is both "close" to the content image and "close" to the style image. Of course, here images being "close" to each other simply means that they minimize some predefined cost/objective function.

Specifically, in this setting of style transfer, we feed each of the three images (content, style and noise) though a pretrained Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) - in this case we used the VGG16 network trained on ImageNet data, and use the output values of each layer to measure how close the images are to each other.

Loosely speaking, we say that the the noise image is close to the content image if the outputs of the convolutional layers earlier on in the network agree, whereas we say that the noise image and the style image are close if the outputs of all the convolutional layers agree. Here when we talk about outputs of the convolutional layers, we really mean the outputs of the convolutional layers found immediately before the pooling layers.

The images below (mobile)/to the right(desktop) show an example of style tansfer in action. My girlfriend is a very talented illustrator/graphic designer. Our anniversary was around the corner and in addition to a nice dinner and some gifts I wanted to give her something more personal, and handful of style transfered images of the two of us blended with some of her artwork seemed like just the thing! In the process I was able to not only give her a more personal gift, but learned some new machine learning concepts along the way! Win-win :)
